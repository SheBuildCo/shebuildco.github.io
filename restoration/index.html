IICRC S500 4th Edition
Water Damage Classification
Accurate classification is the foundation of every drying program. Class determines equipment selection, quantity, and estimated drying time. Category determines contamination level and safety protocols.
Drying Class — Moisture Load
CLASS 01
Minimal Moisture
Least amount of water absorbed by materials. Only a portion of the room is wet. Materials have a low permeance/porosity.
Low evaporation Minimal material
Typical: 1–2 air movers per affected area
CLASS 02
Significant Moisture
Large amount of water. Entire room carpet and cushion may be saturated. Water has wicked up walls less than 60cm (24 in).
Full carpet/pad Wall wicking <60cm
Typical: 1 air mover per 15–20m²
CLASS 03
Greatest Moisture
Greatest amount of water. Water may have come from overhead. Walls, ceilings, insulation and sub-floor materials are saturated.
Ceiling wet Insulation/subfloor Overhead source
High equipment density required
CLASS 04
Specialty Drying
Involves materials with very low permeance/porosity — hardwood, concrete, plaster, brick. Requires longer drying times and specialised equipment.
Hardwood floors Concrete/masonry Desiccant required
Injectidry + desiccant typical
Water Category — Contamination Level
CATEGORY 01
Clean Water
Originates from a sanitary source. Poses no substantial risk from dermal, ingestion, or inhalation exposure. Source examples: broken water supply lines, tub/sink overflow.
Supply line break Appliance overflow Melting ice/snow
CATEGORY 02
Grey Water
Contains significant contamination and has potential to cause discomfort or sickness if ingested. Includes discharge from dishwashers, washing machines, and toilet bowl overflow (no faeces).
Washing machine Toilet bowl (urine only) PPE required
CATEGORY 03
Black Water
Grossly contaminated. Contains pathogenic, toxigenic, or other harmful agents. Causes significant adverse reactions. Includes sewage, flooding from seawater, rising water from rivers/streams, wind-driven rain, and Category 1 or 2 water that has degraded over time.
Sewage Flood water Full PPE mandatory Category 3 protocols Contents disposal likely
Category Escalation — Important Note
⚠️
Category Can Escalate
Category 1 water left untreated can escalate to Category 2 within 24–48 hours due to microbial growth. Category 2 water can escalate to Category 3 if left untreated. Time of assessment is critical for accurate categorisation and scope documentation.

Ref: IICRC S500 4th Ed. Section 7 — Water Categories
Classification reference only. Drying class and water category must be determined by a qualified water damage restoration professional using appropriate moisture detection equipment and psychrometric evaluation on site.
